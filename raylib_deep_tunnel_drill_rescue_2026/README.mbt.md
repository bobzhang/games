# Deep Tunnel Drill Rescue 2026

Deep Tunnel Drill Rescue 2026 is a tile-based underground exploration and rescue game where the player pilots a drilling vehicle through procedurally generated terrain to locate and extract trapped miners. The game world is a 42x28 grid of 24-pixel tiles displayed on a 1366x768 window, with a base station at the top center and increasingly hazardous terrain below. Tile types include empty space, dirt (drillable), rock (impassable), gas pockets (damaging), crystals (collectible), miners (rescuable), and the base platform.

The core gameplay loop is a resource-management cycle: the player drills downward through dirt tiles to find miners, picks them up (auto-rescue on contact or manual action), and returns to the surface base to deliver them. The base refills fuel (34/s), oxygen (46/s), hull (12/s), and cools heat (18/s extra), and converts every 3 collected crystals into a bomb. The player must rescue at least 16 miners to win, while managing four depleting resources: fuel (consumed by movement at 0.08/s and drilling at 0.06/s), oxygen (drains at 0.92 + tier*0.08/s, faster near gas), hull (damaged by gas proximity and overheating), and heat (generated by drilling at 1.2 per tile, dissipates at 6.2/s).

Difficulty scales dynamically through a tier system (tier = 1 + rescued/5, max 10). Higher tiers increase oxygen drain, hazard event frequency, and the number of cave-ins and gas pockets spawned by periodic hazard events (every 5.2-9.2 seconds minus tier*0.1). The game features sonar scanning to locate the nearest miner, bombs that clear a 5x5 diamond of tiles (but kill miners caught in the blast, causing score penalties), and a 360-second mission timer. The game ends on mission success (16+ miners rescued), or failure from hull/oxygen depletion, timer expiry, or when remaining miners become insufficient to reach the target.

## Build and Run

```bash
moon build --target native raylib_deep_tunnel_drill_rescue_2026/
./_build/native/debug/build/raylib_deep_tunnel_drill_rescue_2026/raylib_deep_tunnel_drill_rescue_2026.exe
```

## Controls

- **W / Up Arrow**: Move drill up
- **A / Left Arrow**: Move drill left
- **S / Down Arrow**: Move drill down
- **D / Right Arrow**: Move drill right
- **J / Space**: Drop bomb (clears nearby tiles in 5x5 diamond)
- **K / H**: Activate sonar (reveals nearest miner location)
- **L / R**: Manual rescue action (pick up adjacent miners)
- **Enter**: Start game / Continue after win/loss
- **F11**: Toggle fullscreen
- **On-screen D-pad / buttons**: Touch-friendly movement and actions (mouse click supported)

## How to Play

Pilot the yellow drill vehicle using WASD or arrow keys. The drill moves with physics-based acceleration (380 px/s^2) and drag (5.4), capped at 210 px/s maximum speed. Moving into dirt tiles automatically drills through them, consuming fuel and generating heat. Rock tiles are impassable and must be cleared with bombs. Gas tiles (green) damage hull and accelerate oxygen depletion when the player is in adjacent cells.

Miners are scattered throughout the deep terrain (placed at rows 8-25). Move adjacent to a miner tile to automatically pick them up, or press L/R for manual rescue. You can carry up to 4 miners at a time. Return to the blue BASE platform at the top center to deliver carried miners (adding 58 score each), refill all resources, and convert collected crystals into bombs (3 crystals = 1 bomb, max 6 bombs).

Use sonar (K/H) to ping the nearest miner -- a green line shows the direction and distance for 2 seconds, with a 2.2-second cooldown. Use bombs (J/Space) to blast a diamond-shaped area around you, clearing rock, dirt, gas, and crystals -- but beware, miners caught in the blast become casualties (costing 40 score each) and may make the mission impossible.

Periodic hazard events (every 5.2-9.2 seconds) spawn cave-ins (empty tiles become rock) and new gas pockets, with frequency increasing at higher tiers. The game is won by rescuing 16 or more miners. It is lost if hull, oxygen, or the 360-second timer reaches zero, or if the remaining miners plus carried miners cannot reach the target of 16.

## Architecture

### Package Structure

```
raylib_deep_tunnel_drill_rescue_2026/
├── main.mbt    -- Complete game: structs, map generation, physics, drilling, rescue, rendering, HUD
└── moon.pkg    -- Package config (single main package, imports math + raylib)
```

This is a single-file game with all state managed as local variables in the `main` function. A `reset_run` closure handles game initialization and map regeneration. The game loop processes input, physics, resource management, entity interactions, hazard events, win/loss conditions, and rendering sequentially.

### Data Flow

1. **Input**: WASD/Arrow keys read via `@raylib.is_key_down` for movement (held), `@raylib.is_key_pressed` for actions (pressed once). Mouse position checked against on-screen button rectangles for touch support. Unified into boolean flags (`move_l`, `move_r`, `move_u`, `move_d`, `bomb_action`, `sonar_action`, `rescue_action`).
2. **Movement & Drilling**: Acceleration (380 px/s^2) applied from input, drag (5.4) decays velocity, speed capped at 210 px/s. `try_move_axis` resolves collisions per-axis: dirt tiles are drilled (removed) if fuel > 0.15 and heat < 96% max, rock tiles block movement, other tiles allow passage. Fuel consumed on movement (0.08/s) and drilling (0.06/s extra + 0.18 per tile).
3. **Resources**: Oxygen drains continuously (0.92 + tier*0.08/s, plus 0.20/s per adjacent gas tile). Gas tiles also deal hull damage (0.45/s per adjacent gas). Heat dissipates at 6.2/s; exceeding heat_max causes 5.0 hull damage/s. At base: fuel refills at 34/s, oxygen at 46/s, hull at 12/s, heat cools at 18/s extra.
4. **Rescue**: Miners are picked up by proximity (standing on miner tile) or manual action (L/R key, checks 3x3 neighborhood). Carried miners (max 4) are delivered at base for 58 score each. Crystal collection adds 24 score; 3 crystals convert to 1 bomb at base (max 6 bombs).
5. **Hazards**: Periodic events (5.2-9.2s interval, decreasing with tier) spawn cave-ins and gas pockets. Bombs clear a diamond area but may kill miners (40 score penalty each). Gas proximity drains oxygen and hull. Overheating damages hull.
6. **Win/Loss**: Win when rescued_total >= 16. Lose when hull <= 0, oxygen <= 0, timer <= 0, or remaining miners + carried < 16 (impossible to win).
7. **Rendering**: Background gradient, tiles (color-coded by type with animation), base marker, player (drill vehicle with wobble), sonar line (fading green), particles, HUD panel (resource bars + counters), touch UI, status messages, and overlay screens for menu/win/loss.

### Key Design Patterns

- **Tile-Based World**: The entire game world is a flat `Array[Int]` of 42*28 = 1176 elements, with integer tile type constants. Grid-to-world and world-to-grid conversion functions bridge pixel coordinates and tile indices.
- **Object Pool Pattern**: Particles use a pre-allocated array of 420 slots with `active` flags, scanned linearly for the first free slot on spawn.
- **Per-Axis Collision Resolution**: Movement is resolved independently for X and Y axes via `try_move_axis`, preventing diagonal drilling exploits and enabling smooth wall sliding.
- **Closure-Based Reset**: The `reset_run` closure captures all mutable game state variables, allowing a clean restart without a game state struct.
- **Dynamic Difficulty (Tier System)**: Tier scales with rescued miners (tier = 1 + rescued/5, max 10), affecting oxygen drain rate, hazard event frequency, and hazard intensity.
- **Risk-Reward Bombing**: Bombs are a powerful clearing tool but can cause miner casualties, creating a strategic tension between clearing paths efficiently and preserving rescue targets.
- **Touch-Keyboard Parity**: All actions have both keyboard bindings and clickable screen regions (D-pad + action buttons), merged into unified boolean flags before game logic.

## Improvement & Refinement Plan

1. **Extract game state into a struct**: The `main` function uses 15+ mutable local variables (state, timer, rescued_total, casualties_total, crystals_total, tier, score, event_cd, msg, msg_t, scene_t) alongside the Player struct. Consolidating these into a `Game` struct would eliminate the need for the closure-based reset pattern and enable separating update/render into standalone functions.

2. **Use an enum for tile types**: The seven tile types are represented as integer constants (`tile_empty = 0` through `tile_base = 6`). Converting to a proper enum would provide type safety, enable pattern matching, and prevent invalid tile values.

3. **Add depth-based difficulty scaling**: Currently, tile generation uses uniform probabilities regardless of depth. Making deeper rows contain more rock and gas (and higher-value crystals) would create a natural difficulty gradient that rewards deeper exploration.

4. **Implement fog of war or limited visibility**: The entire map is visible from the start. Adding a visibility radius around the player (with sonar extending it temporarily) would increase the exploration tension and make sonar more valuable as a strategic tool.

5. **Add miner rescue urgency**: Trapped miners are static and never expire. Adding a per-miner oxygen timer or periodic hazard damage to miner tiles would create time pressure for each individual rescue, forcing the player to prioritize nearby miners.

6. **Separate update and render phases**: The current 730-line game loop in `main` interleaves input handling, physics, game logic, and rendering. Extracting `update(game, dt)` and `render(game)` functions would improve readability and make it easier to add features like replays or networked play.

7. **Add visual minimap**: With a 42x28 grid, the world extends beyond the visible area context. A small corner minimap showing tile types, player position, and known miner locations would improve navigation without reducing the exploration challenge.

8. **Scale bomb blast radius with depth**: The fixed 5x5 diamond blast could be less effective in deeper, harder terrain (more rock). Alternatively, adding a "heavy bomb" upgrade that costs more crystals but clears a larger area would create meaningful resource decisions.
